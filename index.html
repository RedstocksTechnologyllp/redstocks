Redstocks Adaptive · Investor Research

The algorithm powering
the terminal's intelligence.

The Terminal's FCS scoring and macro regime signals don't come from rules. They come from a trained agent. Three PPO architectures, five years of NIFTY50 data, three distinct market regimes tested. V2 is validated. V4 is in active development. The destination is an autonomous agent swarm that executes on live capital without human intervention.

Stage 1 · Validated
Adaptive V2
PPO agent with 10-day memory window trained on 46 NIFTY50 stocks across 500,000 timesteps. State space: price (close, high, low), MACD, RSI, Bollinger Bands, SMAs (20/50/100/200), Volume Oscillator, ATR. Rotates between equity and cash without any hardcoded rules. Sharpe 1.130 over five years of live test data including a full bear market cycle.
Bear market Sharpe +0.734 versus NIFTY50's +0.335. Regime rotation worked exactly as designed.
COVID crash Sharpe −1.470, worst RL model. 10-day window confirmed the crash too late. Known failure. Published.
Stage 2 · Building Now
Sprinter V4
Second PPO specialist with a 3 to 5 day memory window, purpose built to catch rapid regime shifts that V2's 10-day window misses. V2 handles slow-moving regimes. V4 handles fast ones. Running both in parallel covers the full regime spectrum.
V3 taught us complexity is the enemy. V4 is narrow by design. One fix, one specialist. Nothing added that doesn't earn its place.
Stage 3 · Roadmap
Autonomous Agent Swarm
All agents operating as one system, connected directly to a broking firm's platform via API. Scores, allocates, executes, rebalances, and repositions autonomously. Configurable to individual risk preferences and return objectives.
The Terminal proves the intelligence works. The swarm is that intelligence running at execution speed, on live capital.
V2 with 10-day memory won overall at Sharpe 1.130. In the 2022 bear market it returned +0.734 versus NIFTY50's +0.335. The 10-day window let the agent confirm bearish regime and rotate to cash. V1's single-day memory could not do this.
V2 failed the 2020 COVID crash at Sharpe −1.470, the worst RL model. The 10-day window confirmed the crash only after the market had bottomed. This is the known failure mode. We publish it because an investor who understands the failure is more useful than one who doesn't.
V3 was over-engineered and returned Sharpe 0.784. Fine-tuning V2 with a drawdown penalty changed what "good" meant mid-training — conflicting with 500k steps of already-learned behaviour. VIX and NIFTY features also expanded the state space after weights were set. V2's simplicity beat V3 in almost every regime. V4 Sprinter avoids this by training from scratch on a narrower problem.
